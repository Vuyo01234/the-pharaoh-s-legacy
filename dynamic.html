Pharaoh Rameses II Turnaround

This anatomical and costume turnaround captures the royal persona of Pharaoh Rameses II, also known as Rameses the Great. The design weaves hand-painted ornamental motifs with historical relics from the nineteenth dynasty.

Artistic Directives

  • Nemes Headdress: Styled with classic indigo and gold bands, symbolizing royal divinity and solar alignment.
  • Uraeus (Sacred Cobra): Positioned at the brow to represent sovereign authority, protective strength, and readiness.
  • Shendyt Kilt: Gathered in fine symmetrical pleating to match traditional Old and Middle Kingdom royal statuary.
  • Jeweled Collar (Wesekh): Embellished with lapis lazuli, turquoise, and carnelian bead sequences.

Design Process

The proportions follow Kemet's canonical grid system of eighteen squares from sole to hairline, achieving timeless elegance and imperial stature.
